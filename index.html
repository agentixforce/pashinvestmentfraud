üé≠

Brokers like PASH Investment promise to share their commission with buyers to make deals seem more attractive. They then disappear or provide fake cheques.

üìÑ

Fake Documentation

Fraudulent agents provide forged signatures, incorrect dates, and invalid financial instruments to avoid honoring agreements.

üîÑ

Bait and Switch Tactics

Initial attractive terms are changed after payments are made, pressuring buyers to accept unfavorable conditions.

üè¢

Ghost Companies

Firms operate without proper licensing, using impressive names and fake credentials to appear legitimate.

üí∏

Advance Fee Fraud

Demanding upfront payments for "exclusive deals" or "guaranteed approvals" that never materialize.

üåê

NRI Targeting

Specifically targeting Non-Resident Indians who cannot easily pursue legal action from abroad.

üõ°Ô∏è How to Protect Yourself

1

Verify Broker Credentials

  • Check RERA registration for agents and projects
  • Verify company registration with ROC
  • Ask for official business licenses
  • Research company history and reviews
2

Document Everything

  • Keep written records of all promises
  • Record phone conversations (if legal)
  • Photograph all documents before signing
  • Maintain email trails of communications
3

Independent Verification

  • Visit the property site independently
  • Verify with the builder directly
  • Get second opinions from other agents
  • Consult with real estate lawyers
4

Payment Safety

  • Never pay cash - always use traceable methods
  • Make payments directly to builders, not brokers
  • Avoid advance payments for "exclusive deals"
  • Get receipts for every transaction

  • GST registration

  • Office lease agreement
  • Bank account details (business account)

Red Flag Indicators

  • Reluctance to provide documents
  • Pressure for immediate decisions
  • Commission sharing offers
  • Demands for cash payments
  • No physical office address
  • Unprofessional communication
